// 10. guided-not-direct — design owns the refusal to hand over the answer

When the learner could try first, the design must visibly refuse.

Three patterns. The first thing the learner sees is their own text reflected back, with the friction marked, and an invitation to look again. The reveal mechanic — inline input, hint cascade, or quiet ghost link — is what visually distinguishes a teaching surface from a chatbot.

// 10.A try-first, then reveal

// 10.B hint cascade — three tap-to-reveal layers
9:41
Lernsaathi
Why is in der Küche not in die Küche?
grammatik samajhna
Hint 1
Sochiye: yeh sentence movement dikhata hai, ya state dikhata hai?
Aur hint dekhein
Akkusativ = movement (kahan ja rahe hain).
Dativ = state (kahan ho).
Pura jawab dekhein
Ich gehe in die Küche.
Main kitchen mein ja raha hoon — Akkusativ.
Ich koche in der Küche.
Main kitchen mein hoon aur kuch kar raha hoon — Dativ.
// 10.C diagnosis before correction (writing tasks)
9:41
Lernsaathi
Gestern ich bin nach Berlin gefahren mit meine Familie. Wir haben besucht das Museum und danach wir sind gegangen ins Restaurant.
phir se dekhein

Aapke text mein yeh part dhyaan dene wala hai:

Gestern ich bin gefahren ... wir haben besucht das Museum ...

Iska reason kya ho sakta hai? Ek minute lijiye, phir bataein.

"The app refuses to hand over an answer the learner could attempt themselves; the design must give that refusal a visible shape."
"The same target word stays anchored across question, answer, revision and mistake list — the eye never loses the thread of what's being learned."
"The learner should feel calm and supported, never measured."
"The interface should carry the bilingual respect inward — German and Hinglish as equals, neither dominating, neither apologizing."
